Our courses

International Foundation Programme

24-week Foundation degree preparing you for university.
The International Foundation Programme is a 30 week programme designed for students who wish to study at Middlesex University Dubai but who may not meet the necessary entry requirements for direct entry onto one of our undergraduate programmes. The IFP prepares students for enrolment onto one of our many Bachelor’s Degrees and allows them to settle into life on campus and bridge the gap between high school and university education. Students can choose to specialise in Business, Law and Politics, Psychology, Arts and Media, or Engineering and Computer Informatics.

Business

Careers-focused degrees at a leading UK Business School in Dubai.

The Business School has been a major provider of business, management and professional accredited programmes for more than a decade, with a sound record of working in partnership with public and private sector organisations within the UAE. Designed to meet the needs of employers and organisations, students develop into professionals who are able to manage ethically, sensitively and holistically across a range of sectors.

Accounting and Finance

Specialist Accounting education recognised by industry
Our Accounting and Finance programmes are recognised globally as offering excellent preparation for professionals in the field. Our team consists of a diverse group of internationally qualified and renowned scholars and practitioners who specialise in key discipline areas including management accounting, banking regulations, corporate finance, econometrics, data analytics and many more. The department puts a strong emphasis on employability and we have developed strong industry links. Our expert academic staff consult for leaders in business and government. Our learning facilities are state-of-the-art and we have received recognition of our programmes from some of the world’s leading professional bodies including the ACCA, UAECA, CIMA, CFA, CIPFA, GARP and ICAEW. We aim to produce leading research that impacts policy and practice.

Health and Education

Discover how education defines the world around us.
Middlesex University Dubai’s School of Health and Education covers many areas of study including sociology, leadership, management, curriculum studies, best practice and child developments. Students are encourage to self-reflect and be able to identify and articulate their personal competencies through the many varied opportunities provided, along with the benefit of studying in a rich, diverse and culturally stimulating setting. Graduates of these programmes hold a well-developed selection of skills including communication, problem-solving and teamwork.

Psychology

Gain an applied knowledge of human behaviour and modern psychology.
The Department of Psychology offers a solid grounding in modern psychology through a mixture of theoretical and professional practice, offering the opportunity to specialise in a range of pathways. Our students are encouraged to consider how psychology interacts with other academic disciplines such as Human Resource Management, Marketing, Education and Criminology, equipping them to pursue global careers in a range of industries where an applied knowledge of human behaviour and experiences will ensure they stand out to potential employers. Students also gain exposure to the world of clinical , counseling, health, and industrial psychology and develop the counselling and interpersonal skills needed to undertake further postgraduate training to practice as clinical, counselling, health, and industrial psychologists.

Computer Engineering and Informatics

Drive how new technologies are changing our society.
Drive how new technologies are changing our society.The Department of Computer Engineering and Informatics at Middlesex University Dubai encompasses modern engineering, technology and computing taught by award-winning faculty with decades of collective industry and academic experience. Our students are given an edge through partnerships with leading STEM organisations in the Middle East and around the world and are able to pursue coveted industry qualifications as part of their curriculum. Through leading research conducted by our Centre for Innovation in Human Experience, students are also exposed to cutting-edge technologies and concepts such as Artificial Intelligence, Robotics and Data Science during their studies, equipping them to pursue global, impactful careers in this in-demand field. We invite you to read our annual newsletter, MDX.STREAM, which highlights the our department’s activities.

Media

Redefine how the world communicates & consumes media
Recognised as the ‘Best Media Centre’ in the region by Forbes Middle East (2019), Middlesex University Dubai’s School of Media directly responds to the ever-changing ways in which we communicate. These vibrant programmes are taught by expert faculty with an array of industry experience, bring a diverse range of skillsets and knowledge to the classroom. Students are encouraged to participate in internships, field trips and competitions, and have won various awards at events such as the Dubai Lynx Festival of Creativity and the Dubai Film Festival.

Art and Design

Innovative Graphic Design and Fashion Design education
The School of Art and Design at Middlesex University Dubai comprises of Graphic Design and Fashion Design. The programmes are taught in state-of-the-art design labs and our newly built Fashion Studio featuring its very own runway. Students are exposed to a variety of design mediums and encourage to build their technical skills and discover their design identity.

Law

Cutting-edge British Law degrees in the heart of Dubai.
The School of Law at Middlesex University Dubai is underpinned by cutting-edge research and delivered by scholars and academics with world-class reputations, who bring a wealth of knowledge and international experience to the classroom. Students are challenged to think beyond the textbook by analysing legal principles and applying them to practical situations, assessing competing arguments and choosing between alternative approaches. Alongside developing a thorough grounding in the core areas of English law, graduates gain the legal skills and academic qualifications to embark on the next stage of training or enter related professions.

24-Hour Cyclothon: MDX Dubai Joins Second Edition of the Triple-Campus Brit Challenge

Friday, February 27, 2026

Middlesex University Dubai together with Middlesex University London and Middlesex University Mauritius, came together for the second edition of the Middlesex Triple-Campus Brit Challenge, taking part in a 24-hour cyclothon that connected the three campuses through sport, teamwork and friendly competition.The challenge saw participants cycling on stationary bikes continuously for 24 hours, with each campus working to accumulate as many kilometres as possible. The event highlighted the strong sense of community across the global Middlesex network.This year’s challenge saw impressive distances recorded across all three campuses, with Middlesex University Dubai cycling a remarkable 4683.95km. Middlesex University Mauritius recorded 4511.39km, while Middlesex University London reached 3190km.At MDX Dubai, a total of 200 students and staff took part in the 24-hour event, cycling in shifts throughout the day and night to contribute to the campus’ outstanding performance. Demonstrating the strong momentum of the challenge, Dubai reached the milestone of 2026km in just 11 hours, highlighting the dedication and endurance of participants.Several individual performances stood out during the event. Among students, Harsh Nichani recorded the longest distance with 163km, followed by Romeo Rimui with 150km and Haneef with 143km. A memorable moment came when Haneef chose to break his Ramadan fast while continuing to cycle, showing determination and commitment to the challenge.Other notable student distances included Morgan Kalya (140km), Richard Daniel (126.6km), Juac Agor (125km), Rohan (125km), Moustafa (120km), James Landicho (103km) and Akshara Murugan (100km).Among staff participants, Coach Farhan Firoz, Head Coach of Middlesex University’s Athletics Team recorded the highest distance with 122km, followed by Seán Bambrick, Manager - Student Recruitment, Global Partnerships and Outreach, completed an impressive 112km in a single ride, making him the only rider to surpass the 100km mark in a single attempt, followed by Dr Paul Read, Associate Professor in Sports Science & Strength and Conditioning, with 103.1km and Sasa Obradovic, Sports and Fitness Coordinator at the London Sport Institute in Dubai with 102km. Additional strong performances came from Coach Slavko Micanovic, Student Activities Manager (85km), Gabriele Poniskaityte, Assistant Manager Student Activities (81.1km), Dr Krishnadas Nanath, Associate Professor of Data Science and Artificial Intelligence and Deputy Head, Computer Engineering and Informatics (CEI) (70.1km) and Mohamed Shahid, Assistant Manager Student Recruitment (45km).The event also saw strong participation from sports teams across the university. The MDX Dubai Athletics Team recorded the highest team contribution, cycling an impressive 1600km during the challenge.Building on the success of its second edition, the Triple-Campus Brit Challenge continues to grow as an annual initiative bringing together students and staff across Middlesex campuses through sport, collaboration and friendly competition.Highlights from the event can be viewed on the @wearemdx Instagram page.

CAA (MoHESR) Accreditation Milestone for Middlesex University Dubai

Thursday, February 26, 2026

Middlesex University (MDX) Dubai has reached a historic milestone, with all undergraduate and postgraduate programmes now accredited by the Commission for Academic Accreditation (CAA) of the Ministry of Higher Education and Scientific Research (MoHESR).This achievement marks the successful completion of a key stage of the accreditation process, confirming that all 90+ bachelors honours degrees and postgraduate programmes currently open for recruitment for the 2026–27 academic year meet federal regulatory standards.What this Stage of Accreditation MeansMDX Dubai was granted its institutional licence by the CAA in January 2025: the first stage in the UAE’s federal accreditation framework. The second stage required the detailed review and approval of every individual academic programme.With this milestone now achieved, the University stands accredited at both institutional and programme level under MoHESR. This is in addition to the longstanding recognition of our programmes by the Dubai Government’s Knowledge and Human Development Authority (KHDA).For students, this means:Moving forward, all current undergraduate and postgraduate degrees are nationally recognised in the UAE.Qualifications meet federal academic standards set by MoHESR.Graduates benefit from enhanced academic credibility locally and internationally.For the institution, it affirms academic rigour, quality assurance systems, governance standards, and compliance with one of the region’s most robust regulatory frameworks. MDX Dubai as a branch campus of Middlesex University in London, operates as part of a global academic network, with students graduating with a Middlesex University London degree awarded under the same academic regulations and quality assurance frameworks as the UK campus. This ensures parity of standards, international recognition, and access to a wider global alumni and industry network. A Landmark Year for MDX DubaiThe accreditation milestone comes during a year of extraordinary growth and transformation for the University.Over the past academic cycle, MDX Dubai has:Expanded its academic portfolio with future-focused programmes aligned to the UAE’s economic and innovation agendas.Launched the London Sport Institute in Dubai, strengthening its position within the region’s rapidly developing sports ecosystem.Continued to grow its student community, maintaining its position as the largest UK university in Dubai.Delivered record graduation ceremonies celebrating the achievements of the Class of 2025.Hosted two of the largest career fairs in the university’s history, encouraging employability for students and alumni. This moment consolidates the university’s standing not only as a long-established and the largest international branch campus in Dubai that is approved by the KHDA, but additionally as a federally accredited higher education institution within the UAE.Looking AheadCAA accreditation signals stability, quality and long-term commitment. It reinforces MDX Dubai’s dedication to delivering internationally benchmarked education that is recognised both locally by the Ministry of Higher Education and Scientific Research (MoHESR) and globally.As the University prepares for the 2026–27 academic year, this milestone provides confidence to students, parents, industry partners and alumni alike: our programmes meet the highest national standards.It is a defining chapter in the university’s journey, and a foundation for an even more ambitious future.

MOU Signing Ceremony: Middlesex University Dubai Strengthens Industry Partnerships to Advance Future Skills

Monday, February 23, 2026

Middlesex University (MDX) Dubai hosted a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) signing ceremony on 19 February 2026 at the MDX Innovation Hub, formalising strategic partnerships with Pearl Initiative, Drone360, SpacePoint, and Lab of Future.The agreements reinforce the University’s commitment to industry-integrated education by expanding opportunities for students to engage with real-world challenges, emerging technologies, and future-focused skills development.The ceremony brought together academic leaders and industry representatives, with welcome remarks delivered by Professor Fehmida Hussain, Deputy Director Engagement and Student Experience, Head of the Computer Engineering and Informatics department, and Head and Founder of the MDX Innovation Hub (MIH), followed by partnership introductions and signing sessions with each organisation.Professor Fehmida Hussain said:“Today, we strengthen our commitment to industry-integrated education. Through our partnerships with Pearl Initiative, Drone360, SpacePoint, and Lab of Future, we are expanding real-world learning, ethical leadership, emerging technologies, and future skills, ensuring our students graduate not only qualified, but truly future-ready.”Strengthening Industry CollaborationEach partnership supports a distinct area of innovation and professional development:Pearl InitiativeThe collaboration with Pearl Initiative focuses on promoting ethical leadership, governance, and responsible innovation. The partnership will help students understand the importance of accountability and transparency as key foundations of sustainable business practice.Drone360Through its partnership with Drone360, MDX Dubai will advance experiential learning in autonomous systems and drone technologies. Students will benefit from live demonstrations, industry workshops, internships, and exposure to real-world applications across infrastructure, logistics, and smart city development.SpacePointThe agreement with SpacePoint strengthens the University’s engagement in satellite development and advanced STEM education. The collaboration provides students with opportunities for hands-on learning in aerospace technologies, innovation projects, and space-related disciplines.Lab of FutureThe partnership with Lab of Future supports the development of future-ready skills through masterclasses, hackathons, innovation challenges, and professional development initiatives, encouraging creativity and interdisciplinary collaboration.Driving Future-Ready EducationThese partnerships reflect Middlesex University Dubai’s continued focus on bridging academia and industry, equipping students with practical experience, professional networks, and the skills required to succeed in rapidly evolving global industries.By working closely with leading organisations, MDX Dubai continues to provide innovative learning environments that prepare graduates to contribute meaningfully to the future workforce and knowledge economy.
